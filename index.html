About Butterflies

Butterflies are not just beautiful insects—they play several critical roles in ecosystems and benefit the world in ways that might surprise you.

Basic Facts

  • Classification: Order Lepidoptera.
  • Life Cycle: Egg → Caterpillar → Pupa → Adult.
  • Diet: Adults drink nectar; caterpillars eat leaves.

Roles & Benefits

  • Pollination: They help plants reproduce and reach flowers bees cannot.
  • Food Source: Essential prey for birds, spiders, and insects.
  • Environmental Indicators: Sensitive to climate and habitat changes.
  • Plant Diversity: Support genetic diversity through pollination.
  • Cultural Importance: Inspire art, science, and ecotourism.

Fun Facts

  • Some species migrate thousands of miles, like the Monarch butterfly.
  • Bright colors can warn predators or act as camouflage.
